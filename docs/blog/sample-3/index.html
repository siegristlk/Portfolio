The learning unit focuses on the replication crisis in empirical research, which poses a dilemma that questions the fundamental principles of scientific research. In view of the importance of knowledge and discovery, the reliability and replicability of research results is important, as it has a strong impact on the relevance and generalizability of the results. The replication crisis, characterised by the lack of replicability of research results, affects all disciplines and scientists, researchers and data users alike (Open Science Collaboration, 2015). Diener & Biswas-Diener (2023) conclude that it is important for both researchers and users of scientific knowledge to understand phenomena such as replicability in order to assess whether results are consistent with reality and to understand the impact of research decisions on the quality of results. The learning unit aims to introduce the importance of the replication crisis, to highlight its relevance for academic work and to provide an insight into the dilemmas that prevail. Moreover, learners will be able to manage the impact of research decisions according to the principles of transparency and replicability by promoting understanding and knowledge transfer. As ID-model, Gang√©’s nine instructional events was choosen provides a strong foundation for the unit. The model includes all essential aspects of learning: Presenting knowledge, providing practice with feedback, and guiding the learner to ensure an optimal learning experience (Gagn√©, 1985). “The purpose of these events is to activate and support the internal processes of learning” (Gagn√© et al., 1992, p. 202). This is particularly important for developing the learning unit and for considering how learners can receive, process, and integrate the information into meaningful cognitive knowledge structures. In addition, the model allows for the creation of instructions in multiple application domains, which increases the adaptability to the learning unit (Gagn√© & Driscoll, 1988). Moreover, the 9 events of instruction serve to support external events, a crucial element for utilizing Goal Based Scenarios (Schank, 1994) to build an interactive learning environment. It effectively introduced the concept of replication crisis, highlighted its significance and challenges, and presented strategies to address its impact. The learning objectives were achieved, as learners should now understand the replication crisis, be able to identify and explain questionable research practices, and identify ways to address the challenges. The information is presented in a clear and concise format. The unit was designed to be accessible to both novice and experienced researchers by addressing learners with different levels of prior knowledge and experience.
